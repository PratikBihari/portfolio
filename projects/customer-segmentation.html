Customer Segmentation Analysis

Using clustering techniques to identify distinct customer groups for targeted marketing

Completed: November 2022
Client: E-commerce Platform Inc.
Duration: 2 months
Back to Projects

Project Overview

E-commerce Platform Inc., an online retailer with over 2 million registered customers, was struggling with a one-size-fits-all marketing approach that resulted in low conversion rates and inefficient marketing spend. The company had collected extensive customer data but lacked the analytical framework to transform this data into actionable customer insights.

The client needed a data-driven customer segmentation solution that could identify distinct customer groups with similar behaviors, preferences, and value to the business. This segmentation would enable targeted marketing campaigns, personalized product recommendations, and more efficient allocation of marketing resources.

Technical Approach

I implemented a comprehensive customer segmentation solution using advanced analytics and machine learning techniques, following these key steps:

Data Collection & Preparation

I gathered and integrated customer data from multiple sources, including:

  • Transaction history (purchases, returns, cart abandonment)
  • Browsing behavior (page views, time spent, search queries)
  • Customer demographics (age, gender, location)
  • Customer service interactions
  • Email engagement metrics

After thorough data cleaning and feature engineering, I created a rich customer profile dataset with over 30 behavioral and demographic attributes for each customer.

Exploratory Data Analysis

I conducted extensive exploratory analysis to understand the distribution of key customer attributes and identify potential relationships between variables. This included:

  • Recency, Frequency, Monetary (RFM) analysis
  • Purchase pattern analysis across product categories
  • Correlation analysis between customer attributes
  • Outlier detection and treatment

Feature Selection & Dimensionality Reduction

To improve the quality of clustering and reduce computational complexity, I:

  • Selected the most relevant features based on business objectives
  • Applied Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce dimensionality while preserving information
  • Standardized features to ensure equal contribution to the clustering algorithm

Clustering Implementation

I evaluated several clustering algorithms, including:

  • K-means clustering
  • Hierarchical clustering
  • DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering)
  • Gaussian Mixture Models

After comparing their performance using silhouette scores, within-cluster variance, and business interpretability, K-means emerged as the most effective approach for this particular dataset.

# Python code snippet for K-means clustering implementation
import pandas as pd
import numpy as np
from sklearn.preprocessing import StandardScaler
from sklearn.decomposition import PCA
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans
from sklearn.metrics import silhouette_score
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Standardize the features
scaler = StandardScaler()
scaled_features = scaler.fit_transform(customer_features)

# Apply PCA for dimensionality reduction
pca = PCA(n_components=0.95)  # Retain 95% of variance
reduced_features = pca.fit_transform(scaled_features)

# Determine optimal number of clusters using silhouette scores
silhouette_scores = []
for k in range(2, 11):
    kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=k, random_state=42, n_init=10)
    cluster_labels = kmeans.fit_predict(reduced_features)
    silhouette_avg = silhouette_score(reduced_features, cluster_labels)
    silhouette_scores.append(silhouette_avg)
    print(f"For n_clusters = {k}, the silhouette score is {silhouette_avg:.3f}")

# Plot silhouette scores
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
plt.plot(range(2, 11), silhouette_scores, marker='o')
plt.xlabel('Number of clusters')
plt.ylabel('Silhouette Score')
plt.title('Silhouette Score vs. Number of Clusters')
plt.show()

# Apply K-means with optimal number of clusters
optimal_k = 5  # Based on silhouette analysis and business interpretability
kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=optimal_k, random_state=42, n_init=10)
customer_segments = kmeans.fit_predict(reduced_features)

# Add segment labels to original data
customer_data['segment'] = customer_segments

Customer Segments Identified

The analysis revealed five distinct customer segments, each with unique characteristics and business implications:

Segment 1: High-Value Loyalists (18%)

  • Highest average order value ($120+)
  • Purchase frequency: 2+ orders per month
  • Broad category engagement
  • Low price sensitivity
  • High email engagement rates
  • Predominantly urban, age 35-50

Segment 2: Potential Enthusiasts (24%)

  • Moderate average order value ($75-90)
  • Growing purchase frequency
  • Category-specific engagement
  • Responsive to promotions
  • High mobile app usage
  • Predominantly suburban, age 25-40

Segment 3: Occasional Shoppers (31%)

  • Lower average order value ($40-60)
  • Infrequent purchases (1-2 per quarter)
  • Seasonal shopping patterns
  • High price sensitivity
  • Moderate email engagement
  • Diverse demographics

Segment 4: New Customers (15%)

  • Variable order values
  • Limited purchase history (< 3 months)
  • Exploratory browsing behavior
  • High cart abandonment rate
  • Responsive to first-time buyer promotions
  • Skew younger (18-30)

Segment 5: At-Risk Customers (12%)

  • Declining engagement
  • No purchases in last 3+ months
  • Previously regular customers
  • Low email open rates
  • High support ticket history
  • Mixed demographics

Segment-Specific Strategies

Based on the segmentation analysis, I developed tailored strategies for each customer segment:

High-Value Loyalists

For this most valuable segment, I recommended:

  • Premium loyalty program with exclusive benefits
  • Early access to new products and collections
  • Personalized product recommendations based on purchase history
  • VIP customer service channel
  • Referral incentives to leverage their network

Potential Enthusiasts

For this growth-oriented segment, I recommended:

  • Category expansion incentives to broaden their engagement
  • Tiered loyalty program to encourage increased purchase frequency
  • Mobile app-exclusive promotions
  • Cross-selling campaigns based on category affinities

Occasional Shoppers

For this price-sensitive segment, I recommended:

  • Limited-time promotions to create urgency
  • Value-oriented messaging highlighting product benefits
  • Seasonal engagement campaigns aligned with their shopping patterns
  • Bundle offers to increase average order value

New Customers

For this exploratory segment, I recommended:

  • Onboarding email series to educate about product offerings
  • First-time buyer incentives for second purchase
  • Simplified navigation and product discovery features
  • Cart abandonment recovery campaigns

At-Risk Customers

For this declining engagement segment, I recommended:

  • Win-back campaigns with compelling offers
  • Feedback surveys to identify pain points
  • Reactivation incentives based on previous purchase preferences
  • Simplified return/exchange process to address potential service issues

Challenges & Solutions

Throughout the project, several challenges emerged that required innovative solutions:

Data Integration

The client's data was scattered across multiple systems with inconsistent customer identifiers. To address this, I:

  • Developed a robust customer identity resolution framework
  • Implemented data quality checks and cleansing procedures
  • Created a unified customer data schema

Feature Selection

With over 50 potential customer attributes, determining the most relevant features for segmentation was challenging. I addressed this by:

  • Conducting feature importance analysis using Random Forest
  • Applying domain expertise to select business-relevant attributes
  • Testing different feature combinations and evaluating segment quality

Segment Stability

Initial segmentation attempts produced unstable clusters that changed significantly with small data variations. To improve stability, I:

  • Implemented ensemble clustering techniques
  • Used bootstrapping to validate cluster robustness
  • Developed a hybrid approach combining RFM analysis with behavioral clustering

Actionability

Ensuring the segments were not just statistically valid but also actionable for marketing teams was crucial. I addressed this by:

  • Conducting workshops with marketing stakeholders to refine segment definitions
  • Creating detailed segment personas with clear marketing implications
  • Developing segment-specific strategy playbooks

Conclusion

The customer segmentation project transformed E-commerce Platform Inc.'s approach to customer engagement, shifting from a mass-marketing strategy to a targeted, personalized approach. By identifying distinct customer segments and their unique characteristics, the company was able to tailor its marketing efforts, product recommendations, and customer service approaches to meet the specific needs of each group.

The success of this project led to an expanded engagement with the client, including the development of a real-time segmentation engine that dynamically updates customer segments based on behavioral changes, enabling even more responsive and personalized customer experiences.
