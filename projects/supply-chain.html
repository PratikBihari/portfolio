Supply Chain Optimization

2021 Inventory Management Lead Data Analyst

Project Overview

For a major retail client facing challenges with excess inventory and associated carrying costs, I developed a data-driven inventory optimization model. The solution helped the client reduce inventory levels while maintaining service levels, resulting in significant cost savings.

Business Problem

The client was struggling with:

  • Excess inventory across multiple warehouses
  • High carrying costs affecting profitability
  • Inconsistent inventory levels between locations
  • Lack of data-driven forecasting for inventory planning

Approach & Methodology

  1. Data Collection: Gathered 3 years of historical sales, inventory, and supply chain data from multiple systems
  2. Data Preparation: Cleaned and normalized data across different warehouse management systems
  3. Exploratory Analysis: Identified patterns in inventory turnover, seasonal demand fluctuations, and product lifecycles
  4. Model Development: Created time series forecasting models to predict future demand and optimize reorder points
  5. Implementation: Developed an interactive dashboard for inventory managers to visualize optimal inventory levels

Tools & Technologies

Python (Pandas, NumPy) Time Series Analysis (ARIMA, Prophet) Optimization Algorithms SQL Power BI

Results & Impact

22%
Reduction in excess inventory
$1.2M
Annual savings in carrying costs
99.2%
Maintained service level

Key Challenges & Solutions

Data Integration Issues

Multiple disparate systems made data consolidation difficult. Created ETL pipelines to standardize data formats across systems.

Seasonal Variations

Demand patterns varied significantly across seasons. Implemented advanced time series decomposition to account for seasonality.

Stakeholder Adoption

Inventory managers were hesitant to trust automated systems. Created intuitive dashboards and conducted training sessions to increase adoption.

Conclusions

This project demonstrated how data science and time series analysis can transform inventory management practices. By implementing a data-driven approach, the client was able to significantly reduce costs while maintaining service levels. The optimization model continues to provide value as it learns from new data and adapts to changing market conditions.
