Project Overview

Global Retail Inc., a multinational retail chain with over 500 stores worldwide, was facing challenges with inventory management. They were experiencing both overstocking (leading to increased storage costs and waste) and stockouts (resulting in lost sales and customer dissatisfaction).

The company needed a reliable forecasting system that could predict future sales with high accuracy, allowing them to optimize their inventory levels and improve overall operational efficiency.

Technical Approach

After a thorough analysis of the client's historical sales data spanning 5 years, I implemented a time series forecasting solution using the following approach:

Data Preprocessing

The raw sales data required extensive cleaning and preprocessing to address issues such as missing values, outliers, and inconsistent formatting. I also performed feature engineering to incorporate relevant external factors like seasonality, holidays, and promotional events.

Model Development

I evaluated several time series forecasting models, including:

  • ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average)
  • SARIMA (Seasonal ARIMA)
  • Prophet (Facebook's forecasting tool)
  • LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) neural networks

After comparing their performance on validation data, SARIMA emerged as the most effective model for this specific use case, providing the best balance between accuracy and interpretability.

Challenges & Solutions

Throughout the project, several challenges emerged that required innovative solutions:

Data Quality Issues

The historical sales data contained numerous inconsistencies and gaps, particularly for newer store locations. I developed a robust data imputation strategy using similar store profiles to fill these gaps without introducing bias.

Handling Special Events

Promotional events, holidays, and other special occasions significantly impacted sales patterns but were difficult to incorporate into traditional time series models. I created custom features to capture these effects and integrated them into the forecasting framework.

Model Scalability

With 500+ stores and thousands of products, computational efficiency was a concern. I implemented a hierarchical forecasting approach that balanced accuracy with performance, allowing the system to generate forecasts for all store-product combinations within reasonable time constraints.
